How Has Software Changed The Stock Market

How has software has changed the industry?

The time of people yelling over a giant room at each other and people frantically calling and answering phone calls in order to buy and sell stocks are but a distant memory. Software has forever changed the way the stock market works. The U.S Securities and Exchange Commission addresses the benefits of software run stock exchanges, saying “the commission is mindful of the benefits of increasing use of new technologies for investors and the market, and has encouraged experimentation and innovation”. (U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, 1997). Today, almost all transactions are done by computers, with automation at the forefront of trading. Now with most of the stock market riding on the back of extraordinary complex software, it has seen increases in the “average daily number of trades going through the ceiling.” (ComputerWeekly, 2007). After the 2008 financial crisis, the stock exchange introduced circuit breakers into the market. “These limited the velocity at which share prices could fall before a halt was called to trading in the particular stock.” With this tool, it curbed the instability that came with panic selling. Henceforth, in theory, making the buying and selling of stock more stable. (ComputerWeekly, 2007).


What role does coding play in the development of these applications

According to Finance Train and Robo Garden, Java has the lead when it comes to developing applications that buy and sell on the stock market, with Python and C++ coming in at a close second and third. (RoboGarden, 2018). As stated earlier, the role of these coding languages is to simulate and analyse the stock market and act accordingly to produce a consistent stream of profit. (Finance Train, 2016). When coding is developed correctly for stock exchanges and individual alike, it gives the ability to make split-seconds decisions thousands of times a day without the reliance on human intervention, thus negating the chance of human error.


Why has this industry transformed in recent times due to coding and technology?

Money is the key driving factor when implementing new code and technologies. The faster a program can accurately respond to the stock markets fluctuant behaviour, the higher the chance a business or individual can turn a profit. As the internet grows, information can travel further and faster than ever before, and with the rise of accessible high-end computing power, investment companies and secondary markets can make more informed decisions and amend their software to fit current trends. (U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, 1997).


What might the industry look like ten years from now?

Technology is the centre point of the stock market. According to Business Insider, “With interest rates near rock bottom, macro growth likely to be slow, and geopolitics a wildcard, investors will have to rely on tech innovation to drive future returns.” (Business Insider, 2020). Therefore, with much of today’s technologies relying on software and code, including the stock market, the next ten years will be heavily reliant on technological innovation. “But if the tech industry doesn't drive the innovation needed for stocks to continue to rise in the long run, then ‘the investment case for equities over the long run diminishes considerably’.” (Business Insider, 2020).
